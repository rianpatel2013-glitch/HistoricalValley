About

Area Information

  • Master-planned community: Valley Ranch is a 2,400-acre, master-planned development designed as a comprehensive environment for residents to live, work, and play.
  • Climate: The Summers are very hot and humid, the Winters are cold and windy, and it is partly cloudy year round. Over the course of the year, the temperature typically varies from 38°F to 96°F and is rarely below 26°F or above 102°F.
  • The community is home to a blend of residential areas, recreational facilities, and commercial spaces. Every person, no matter who they are, will find something to enjoy in Valley Ranch. The combination of nature, modern structures, creates an appealing environment for everyone.

Flood Control Infrastructure

  • Since it sits in the floodplain of the elm fork of the trinity river, various infrastructure has been built to manage floodwaters.
  • Levees: Block floodwaters from spilling into the community
  • Scenic canals: The flood-control canals are also a recreational feature, with over five miles of winding walkways and paths for walking, biking, and jogging.
  • Other Infrastructure that helps control floods: Pump Stations and ponds/lakes
  • Flood protection infrastructure, such as levees, can become rusty, eroded or just outdated, that is why regular maintenance is essential. The levee revival process is a mandatory inspection process that ensures private levee owners are maintaining their levee according to FEMA and United States Corps of Engineers standards.
  • Sources:
    1. About IFCD 3 (Irving Flood Control District 3)

Sports legacy

  • Home of champions: From 1985 until 2016, the Dallas Cowboys called Valley Ranch their home, training there for their 3 Super Bowl wins. The practice facility was also used by the Dallas Stars hockey team for many years.
  • Legends Crossing: After the Cowboys' departure, their former headquarters were redeveloped into a residential area now called "Legends Crossing". To honor the team's history, the streets of the new neighborhood are named after Cowboys legends.
  • Preserved history: The stone facade of the original Cowboys building was preserved and incorporated into the entryways of the new Legends Crossing community.
  • Dr. Pepper StarCenter: While the Stars have moved, Valley Ranch still has a connection to hockey through the StarCenter, which offers lessons and leagues for hockey players.
